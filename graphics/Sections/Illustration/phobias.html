Background

The goal of this concentration was to evoke an emotional response of disgust or fear in the viewer. The point of these pieces was not only to aesthetically please but to provoke the audience, to juxtapose humor and lighthearted puns with the irrational aversion of otherwise harmless topics, and to raise awareness of the very real unrealistic fears that many individuals have. Since not everyone has the phobias represented in this concentration, disgust and forlornness (in the case of Athazagoraphobia) is instead used to make the viewer feel aversion towards the subject matter.

Phobias

  • Triskaidekaphobia - Fear of the number 13
  • Coulrophobia - Fear of clowns
  • Aichmophobia - Fear of sharp objects
  • Acousticophobia - Fear of sound
  • Botanophobia - Fear of plants
  • Xenophobia - Fear of aliens
  • Glossophobia - Fear of public speaking
  • Pediophobia - Fear of dolls
  • Trypophobia - Fear of holes
  • Atychiphobia - Fear of failure
  • Scoleciphobia - fear of worms
  • Athazagoraphobia - fear of being forgotten
